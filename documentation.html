Project 1 - Data Visualization
Design Process
The design of this visualization project was made in three parts (Map, Line Plot and Bar Plot). To start with, I made a line plot without any interactions to display the no.of deaths in a selected day. Then included a tooltip to provide a bit of interaction to the visualization.

In the futher step, I used the streets.json file to draw a map and included some landmarks such as Brewery, Work House and Broad Street. On further research, I found names of two other streets on a GitHub project and included those on the map. Then I plotted the location of deaths with a color hue on gender (both the colors are colorblind safe), and plotted the locations of the water pumps. With a bit of help from my friend, I introduced interaction between line plot and map. The interaction is generated by subtracting the deaths till a selected day and deaths till the previous day.

Finally, I plotted a barplot and converted into grouped bar plot by adding another x1-axis as provided in the d3 examples. The colors filled in the bar plot are colorblind safe. At last, I have included legends and introduced similar tool tips in map and barplot.

Rationale of Design Choices
At first, I chose some random colors to provide gender hue in the visualization. But then changed the colors to make them colorblind safe. The colors are choosen from Color Brewer.

The map consumes a lot of space compared to line and bar plot. Moreover, map is the heart of this visualization. So, I decided to have the map first which will have line plot on it's right. This allows the user to interact between line plot and map easily. Below the map, I have included a legend and a barplot on it's right which is interactive with the line plot.

Insights from the Visualization
The main insights from the Visualization are as follows -
  1. The map debunks the theory that cholera is spread through air. Because not every one from the epi-center (broad street) are effected by cholera. People from brewery are not all effected.
  2. The map provide a insight on the epi-center of the disease. Most of the deaths are in and around broad street.
  3. But there are some outliers, people far away from broad street are also effected by the disease. But in a less number. This provides the information that every body who has been in contact with broad street are effected. Thus, disease is no infectious in nature.
  4. The common thing for all the people in the broad street is the water supply (Hand Pump).
